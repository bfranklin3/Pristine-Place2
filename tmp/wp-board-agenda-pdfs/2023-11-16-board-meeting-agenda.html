Annual Meeting and Election of the Pristine Place Homeowners Association (HOA)

We are pleased to announce that our annual HOA meeting and election will take place on November 16th, starting at 7:00 p.m. During this annual gathering, we will conduct the election of Board members and address four significant ballot items that directly impact our community.

Agenda:

  1. Election of New Board Members: Members will vote and welcome elected members to our Board, a vital part of our community’s decision-making process.
  2. Discussion and Voting on Key Community Issues:
    • Cable Bulk Contract: Aimed at enhancing the quality and cost-effectiveness of our cable and internet services, approval of this initiative will influence the HOA fees to ensure we secure a beneficial group rate for the entire community.
    • Fence Guidelines Update: This proposal seeks to diversify acceptable fencing styles and clarify regulations regarding fence placements within our properties. A favorable vote will lead to modifications in our current Covenants, providing clearer, more accommodating guidelines for residents.
  • Golf Cart Policy: In compliance with Florida’s legal statutes, we are proposing comprehensive golf cart rules to safeguard both residents and the HOA from potential liabilities. Endorsement of this policy will necessitate changes in the community’s Covenants, ensuring we uphold legal standards and maintain a safe environment.
  • New Rental/Leasing Rules: To preserve the integrity of Pristine Place as a primarily owner-occupied community, this item aims to prevent investors from acquiring multiple properties within our community, thereby safeguarding the HOA’s autonomy. Approval will formalize this preference in our Covenants, setting clear residential standards.

We encourage all members to participate in this crucial aspect of our community’s governance. Your input is invaluable, and together, we will continue to enhance the quality of life in Pristine Place. For a detailed overview of each ballot item, please refer to the information below.

We look forward to seeing you there.

DETAILS FOR THE BALLOT ITEMS:

 

1. Cable Bulk Contract:
This initiative proposes a community-wide bulk contract providing each home with two set-top boxes (and 1 DVR), Select Video with Entertainment, 900 Mbps internet, and Wi-Fi service. The five-year commitment increases HOA dues by approximately $50 per month (plus tax), yet most residents will see savings between $50 and $200 compared to their current expenses for similar services. Additionally, Spectrum will contribute a “door fee” of $400 per house, totaling approximately $281,000, paid within 90 days following contract execution. This substantial contribution will significantly enhance the HOA’s budgetary flexibility.

2. Fence Guidelines Update:T
The updated guidelines propose several changes. Moving forward, only black vinyl-clad chain-link fences will be permitted, phasing out approval for the green variant. Fences must align with or stand behind the rear building line, including adjustments for structures like cabana pools, third garages, or sizeable architectural extensions. Furthermore, residents will have four new options for black powder-coated aluminum fences, expanding aesthetic choices while maintaining community standards.

3. Golf Cart Policy:
In alignment with our ongoing commitment to safety and compliance, we have established a comprehensive policy for the use of Golf Carts and Low-Speed Vehicles (LSVs) within our community. This policy integrates Florida’s legal statutes and our community’s specific needs to create a safe, enjoyable environment for all residents.

Usage and Operational Guidelines:

  • Permitted Areas: Golf carts and LSVs are restricted to paved roads within our community boundaries. Operation of these vehicles on sidewalks, unpaved surfaces, or any areas not explicitly designated for vehicular traffic is strictly prohibited.
  • Adherence to Traffic Laws: All operators of golf carts and LSVs are required to follow posted traffic regulations, mirroring the legal requirements imposed on standard motor vehicles.
  • Compliance with State Laws: Operation of golf carts and LSVs must be in strict conformity with Florida Statutes Sections 316.212 and 316.2122. These sections pertain to the lawful use of such vehicles on public roads, and any subsequent amendments to these laws should be observed immediately.
  • No Legal Exemptions: Utilization of golf carts and LSVs within community boundaries does not exempt users from broader vehicular laws and ordinances. All applicable legal expectations continue to hold force.
  • Operator Licensing: Only individuals in possession of a valid driver’s license, issued either by the State of Florida or any other authorized state, are permitted to operate these vehicles within the community.

Liability and Community Protection Measures:

  • Waiver of Claims: All persons utilizing golf carts or LSVs in the community, encompassing both drivers and passengers, agree to waive any form of claims against the Association concerning accidents or issues arising from such use.
  • Indemnification Requirement: Owners of the vehicles, along with the Lot owners housing these vehicles, are obligated to indemnify the Association against any claims, expenses, or costs emerging from vehicle use. This indemnity applies even in instances where the Association is found to be partially at fault.
  • Mandatory Registration: In accordance with our Covenants, all golf carts and LSVs must undergo formal registration before their operation on community streets is sanctioned. This process helps maintain a regulated environment that upholds safety and compliance.

Through these measures, we aim to foster a safe, responsible community where the use of golf carts and LSVs contributes positively to the community without compromising legal standards or individual well-being.

4. Proposed Changes to Rental/Leasing Rules in Covenants:
In an effort to maintain the integrity and residential quality of our community, we propose an amendment to the Covenants regarding the rental and leasing of homes. This change is designed to encourage long-term residency, discourage transient occupancy, and prevent potential negative influences by investment groups on community affairs.
Key aspects of the proposed changes include:

Mandatory Occupancy Period:

  • Homeowners are required to occupy their new residence as their primary dwelling for a minimum of one year (twelve months) before they can lease or rent out the property.
  • This measure is intended to strengthen the sense of community and ensure homeowners are truly invested in their homes and neighborhood.

Exceptions to the Rule:

  • Certain circumstances allow for exemptions from the one-year occupancy requirement, specifically:
    • If a homeowner inherits the property through probate proceedings or other forms of inheritance, they may bypass the mandatory occupancy period.
    • If the property title is transferred into a trust, the primary beneficiary of which is the original owner or the owner’s immediate family, the occupancy requirement is waived. However, the beneficiary must maintain their status for the duration of the property’s inclusion in the trust.

Impact on Investment Purchases:

  • The proposal aims to prevent real estate investment groups from acquiring multiple properties within our community without the intention of long-term residence. This practice can lead to a transient population, which may destabilize the community atmosphere and governance.

 Protection of Community Interests:

  • By implementing this rule, the HOA seeks to safeguard the community’s character and quality of life, ensuring that residents are individuals invested in the welfare and continuous improvement of their surroundings.

Residents’ understanding and support of these changes are crucial. These amendments are not designed to be restrictive but rather to protect and enhance the living experience of all community members. By maintaining a stable, long-term resident base, we can ensure the enduring prosperity and cohesiveness of our community.
